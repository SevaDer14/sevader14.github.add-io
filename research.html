What is Weyl Semimetal?

Weyl semimetal is a crystal with specific cunducting properties that originate from quantum interactions between electrons and crystal lattice. Those interactions influence electrons that take part in conductivity to become massless quasiparticles - Weyl fermions. Conductivity with such particles has number of benefits that allow for apllications in spintronics and future computing.

My Research in Physics

While I was employed in Institute of Metal Physics of Russian Academy of Science I was involved in galvanomagnetic study of HgSe single crystals doped with small amounts of transition metal impurities such as Fe, Co and Ni. During the study we observed anomalus galvanomagnetic effects such as extreemley large and linear magnetoresistance, negative longitudinal magnetoresistance and large quantum oscillations in Hall effect. During our research we established that probable explanation for observed effects can be formation of non-centrocymmetric Weyl semimetal phase in our samples.

What is Weyl Semimetal?

In normal metals, such as copper, elementary particles that carry electrical current are electrons. If such is the case we say that Fermi level lies in the conduction band (upward directed parabolas on the picture) of the material or that conduction band is partially filled. In semiconductors like Si or Ge conduction band is empty and thus, in normal conditions they do not conduct current and therefore are insuators. However it is possible with addition of dopants, to either add new electrons to the conduction band or to take some electrons from valence band (downward directed parabolas on the picture). In latter case the conduction of current will happen with qusiparticles call holes (lack of electron).

Untill recently that was it. But since the dicovery of graphene to wich Nobel prize was awarded new elementary particles can be responsible for conducting current in solids. That is possible if conduction an valence band interact and distort each other. Usually there is a sizable gap between them but like on the top picture in some materials they touch each other and form new particales. In case of Graphene it is Dirac fermion, in Weyl semimetas it is Weyl fermion. Such particles do not have mass and therefore conduct current much better, and some times due to crazy quantum effects can form Fermi arcs and other chiral anomalies that can open a door to new computing methods or allow for high-temperature superconductivity.
